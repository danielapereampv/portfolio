03  ·  Systems & Automation · 2025–2026

Turning invisible marketing into measurable revenue

The context

Across roles, the same problem kept appearing: marketing was running, but no one could see what it produced. Broken attribution, manual handoffs, and metrics scattered across a dozen platforms. So I built the plumbing to fix it.

Rebuilt attribution from zero

A lifecycle program was crediting zero conversions to any campaign or flow. I audited the setup, diagnosed the broken loop, and rebuilt it so conversions were correctly credited, creating, for the first time, a reliable line of sight into the revenue marketing generates.

Unified performance dashboards

Built cross-platform dashboards (PostHog and internal tooling) tracking every metric in one place, from paid media to organic, so leadership could see performance and marketing's revenue contribution at a glance instead of stitching together exports.

Automated client intake, no-code

Designed a brief-intake system using tools the team already had: a Google Form feeding a spreadsheet, with Apps Script automatically generating and routing brief emails between the client and the production team. Faster handoffs, fewer dropped requests, zero new software.

The result

Marketing went from a black box to a system where performance, attribution and revenue contribution are measurable and automated, the difference between guessing and knowing.
